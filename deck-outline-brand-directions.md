## Slide 5 — The Inscription: Concept

**Copy:**
> Coinage Caps traces back to Roman inscriptional lettering — forms designed to be carved in stone. Each letter carries the weight of permanence and tradition.
>
> For Studio Linse Hoogervorst, this direction says: we are established. The studio name stretches across the full width, unhurried and authoritative. It communicates heritage, craftsmanship, and quiet confidence — an interior design practice with deep roots.
>
> This is an identity that feels like it has always existed.

**Assets:** The studio name set in Coinage Caps, white on black, full justified — the cleanest lockup.

---

## Slide 6 — The Inscription: On Photography

**Copy:**
> Justified to the image width, the name becomes part of the composition — a title plate for the work beneath it.

**Assets:** 2–3 lockups showing Coinage Caps justified over different interior photographs. Mix a living space, a detail shot, and a wider room view.

---

## Slide 7 — The Inscription: Applications

**Copy:**
> The identity is singular: one form, one weight, one voice. Strength through consistency.

**Assets:** 3–4 lockups: white background, black background, business card proportion, and a larger format (e.g. project signage or publication cover).

---

## Slide 8 — The Inscription: What it communicates

**Copy:**
> **The studio as:** An established practice with heritage and authority
>
> **Strengths:**
> - Immediately elevated and distinctive
> - The justified spacing adds a modern tension to classical letterforms
> - Communicates seriousness and permanence — clients trust this studio
>
> **Considerations:**
> - The identity has one state — it doesn't flex or reduce for digital contexts
> - Favicon, mobile, and social avatar will need a separate mark
> - The system is the typeface itself — if the type doesn't resonate, there's less to build around
> - Risks reading as conservative — a continuation rather than a new chapter

**Assets:** None — text only.

## Slide 9 — Direction B: The Imprint

**Copy:**
> **Direction B — The Imprint**
>
> Typeface: HAL Timezone Mono
> Tone: Technical, contemporary, precise

**Assets:** Section divider, same style as slide 4.

---

## Slide 10 — The Imprint: Concept

**Copy:**
> HAL Timezone Mono is a monospaced serif — every character occupies the same width, like a technical drawing, like a specification sheet, like the precise measurements that define an interior.
>
> For Studio Linse Hoogervorst, this direction says: we are meticulous. The wordmark has a staggered layout — STUDIO offset right, LINSE HOOGERVORST flush left — that creates an asymmetric composition, controlled but not rigid.
>
> The typewriter heritage of the font connects to process: each design decision is deliberate, each detail considered. This is an identity that signals a younger, more contemporary practice — one that respects craft but isn't bound by tradition.

**Assets:** Full wordmark (STUDIO / LINSE HOOGERVORST) in the stagger layout. Large, centred.

---

## Slide 11 — The Imprint: Two States

**Copy:**
> The identity lives in two forms: the full wordmark and the S/LH monogram. The transition is functional — when the work is centre stage, the studio steps aside. When you need the name, it's there in full.
>
> On the website, this happens through scroll: a typewriter deletion that reduces the full name to initials as you move into the portfolio. On hover, the name types itself back.

**Assets:** Full wordmark (left) and S/LH monogram (right), same stagger proportions. Below: the GIF or 3–4 stills showing the animation mid-transition.

---

## Slide 12 — The Imprint: On Photography

**Copy:**
> The stagger layout creates an asymmetric anchor over photography — a compositional element, not just a label. The monogram works at any scale, from a project wall to a browser tab.

**Assets:** 2–3 lockups of the wordmark over interior photographs. Then 2–3 of S/LH at different scales (large on a photo, corner mark, favicon/avatar).

---

## Slide 13 — The Imprint: System Depth

**Copy:**
> The identity isn't just a logo — it's a connected system:
>
> - **Stagger layout** — the offset creates a distinctive silhouette, recognisable even without reading the name
> - **Monogram** — S/LH as a compact mark for digital, signage, embossing
> - **Typewriter animation** — the transition between states reinforces the precision metaphor
> - **Monospaced grid** — the underlying rhythm of the typeface can extend into layouts, spacing, and print
>
> Each element reinforces the others. The system grows with use.

**Assets:** 4 small lockups, one per bullet, arranged in a 2x2 grid. Or a simple diagram showing how the parts connect.

---

## Slide 14 — The Imprint: What it communicates

**Copy:**
> **The studio as:** A precise, contemporary practice — Job's own voice
>
> **Strengths:**
> - A deep system with multiple expressions (wordmark, monogram, animation, grid)
> - Communicates precision and attention to detail — the qualities that define good interior work
> - Scales from favicon to facade — digitally native from the start
> - The animation gives the website a signature moment people remember
> - Signals a new generation — respects heritage without being defined by it
>
> **Considerations:**
> - Monospaced type has a more understated presence than classical serifs
> - The stagger layout needs consistent application to feel intentional
> - The concept asks you to trust the system — the beauty is in how the pieces work together, not in a single hero moment

**Assets:** None — text only.

## Slide 16 — Recommendation

**Copy:**
> Both directions are credible — but they tell different stories about where the studio is going.
>
> The Inscription honours Paul's legacy. It's beautiful, authoritative, and timeless. But it may anchor the studio in the past.
>
> The Imprint is Job's own mark. It's precise, systematic, and built for how people encounter design studios today — on screens first, in print second. The system has depth: a wordmark that reduces to initials, a grid that extends into everything, and an animation that makes the digital identity memorable.
>
> My recommendation is **The Imprint** — not because the past doesn't matter, but because the identity should point forward.

**Assets:** The Imprint wordmark, alone, generous whitespace.
